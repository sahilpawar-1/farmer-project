Rainy Season Crops in Maharashtra

Rice

Thrives in flooded fields and heavy rainfall.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: June to July
  • Fertilizers: 100 kg N + 50 kg P + 50 kg K per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 1200–1500 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 120–150 days
  • Harvest Time: October to November
  • Notes: Requires standing water and warm temperatures.

Soybean

Popular Kharif oilseed crop.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: Mid-June to early July
  • Fertilizers: 30 kg N + 60 kg P per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 450–700 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 90–110 days
  • Harvest Time: September to October
  • Notes: Rich in protein and oil; used in food and industry.

Maize

Grows well in well-drained monsoon soils.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: June to July
  • Fertilizers: 120 kg N + 60 kg P per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 500–800 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 100–110 days
  • Harvest Time: September to October
  • Notes: Used for food, fodder, and biofuel.

Tur (Pigeon Pea)

Deep-rooted legume for monsoon farming.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: June to July
  • Fertilizers: 20 kg N + 40 kg P per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 600–800 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 150–180 days
  • Harvest Time: November to December
  • Notes: Improves soil fertility and drought resistance.

Groundnut

Oilseed crop suited for sandy loam soils.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: June to July
  • Fertilizers: 20 kg N + 40 kg P + 40 kg K per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 500–600 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 110–120 days
  • Harvest Time: October
  • Notes: Fixes nitrogen and improves soil health.

Sesame

Rain-fed oilseed crop with short duration.

More Info
  • Sowing Time: June to July
  • Fertilizers: 30 kg N + 20 kg P per hectare
  • Water Requirement: 300–400 mm total
  • Growth Duration: 90–100 days
  • Harvest Time: September to October
  • Notes: Valuable for edible oil and traditional medicine.
